# Western Philosophical Traditions Index

## Overview

This document provides a comprehensive index of Western philosophical traditions integrated into Form 28. Each tradition is catalogued with its core tenets, key figures, primary texts, and relationships to other schools.

---

## Ancient Philosophy

### Presocratic Philosophy
- **Period**: c. 600-400 BCE
- **Core Inquiry**: The fundamental nature of reality (physis)
- **Key Figures**: Thales, Anaximander, Heraclitus, Parmenides, Democritus
- **Central Concepts**: Arche (first principle), logos, being vs. becoming, atoms
- **Domain Focus**: Metaphysics, cosmology

### Platonic Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `PLATONIC`
- **Founder**: Plato (428-348 BCE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Theory of Forms: Abstract, perfect archetypes underlying reality
  - Knowledge as recollection (anamnesis)
  - The soul's immortality and tripartite structure
  - The Good as highest Form, source of being and knowledge
  - Dialectic as path to truth
- **Key Figures**: Plato, Plotinus, Proclus, Ficino
- **Primary Texts**: Republic, Phaedo, Symposium, Timaeus, Parmenides
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Epistemology, Ethics, Political Philosophy
- **Related Traditions**: Neoplatonism, Augustinian thought, Cambridge Platonists

### Aristotelian Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `ARISTOTELIAN`
- **Founder**: Aristotle (384-322 BCE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Substance and accident, form and matter (hylomorphism)
  - Four causes: material, formal, efficient, final
  - Virtue ethics: excellence (arete) through habituation
  - Eudaimonia (flourishing) as life's ultimate aim
  - The golden mean between extremes
  - Logic as organon (instrument) of knowledge
- **Key Figures**: Aristotle, Thomas Aquinas, Alasdair MacIntyre
- **Primary Texts**: Nicomachean Ethics, Metaphysics, Physics, Categories, De Anima
- **Domains**: Ethics, Metaphysics, Logic, Philosophy of Mind, Political Philosophy
- **Related Traditions**: Scholasticism, Thomism, Virtue Ethics Revival

### Stoicism
- **Tradition ID**: `STOICISM`
- **Founders**: Zeno of Citium (334-262 BCE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Virtue is the only true good; externals are indifferent
  - Live according to nature and reason (logos)
  - Dichotomy of control: distinguish what's up to us vs. not
  - Apatheia: freedom from destructive passions
  - Cosmopolitanism: all humans share in universal reason
  - Acceptance of fate (amor fati)
- **Key Figures**: Zeno, Cleanthes, Chrysippus, Seneca, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius
- **Primary Texts**: Meditations (Marcus Aurelius), Discourses (Epictetus), Letters (Seneca), Enchiridion
- **Domains**: Ethics, Logic, Physics/Metaphysics
- **Related Traditions**: Cynicism, Roman Stoicism, Modern Stoicism

### Epicureanism
- **Period**: c. 300 BCE onwards
- **Founder**: Epicurus
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Pleasure (ataraxia - tranquility) as highest good
  - Atomic materialism
  - Death is nothing to us
  - The gods exist but don't intervene
  - Friendship and moderation
- **Key Figures**: Epicurus, Lucretius
- **Primary Texts**: Letter to Menoeceus, On the Nature of Things (Lucretius)
- **Domains**: Ethics, Metaphysics, Philosophy of Mind

### Skepticism
- **Period**: c. 300 BCE onwards
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Suspension of judgment (epoché) leads to tranquility
  - Arguments can be found for and against any position
  - Appearances vs. reality distinction
- **Key Figures**: Pyrrho, Sextus Empiricus, Arcesilaus, Carneades
- **Domains**: Epistemology

## Medieval Philosophy

### Neoplatonism
- **Period**: c. 200-600 CE
- **Core Tenets**:
  - The One: ineffable source beyond being
  - Emanation: reality flows from the One through Nous to Soul to Matter
  - Return to the One through contemplation
- **Key Figures**: Plotinus, Porphyry, Proclus, Pseudo-Dionysius
- **Primary Texts**: Enneads
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Mysticism

### Scholasticism
- **Period**: c. 1100-1500 CE
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Faith and reason are compatible
  - Systematic use of Aristotelian logic in theology
  - Dialectical method: thesis, objections, response
- **Key Figures**: Anselm, Abelard, Thomas Aquinas, Duns Scotus, William of Ockham
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Theology, Logic

### Thomism
- **Tradition ID**: `THOMISM`
- **Founder**: Thomas Aquinas (1225-1274)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Synthesis of Aristotelian philosophy and Christian theology
  - Natural law ethics grounded in rational human nature
  - Five ways (proofs) for God's existence
  - Analogy of being (analogia entis)
  - Faith perfects but doesn't contradict reason
- **Key Figures**: Thomas Aquinas, Cajetan, Suárez, Maritain, MacIntyre
- **Primary Texts**: Summa Theologica, Summa Contra Gentiles
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Ethics, Theology, Political Philosophy
- **Related Traditions**: Aristotelian, Natural Law Theory

## Early Modern Philosophy

### Rationalism
- **Tradition ID**: `RATIONALISM`
- **Period**: 17th-18th century
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Reason is the primary source of knowledge
  - Innate ideas exist
  - Mathematical/deductive method applies to philosophy
  - A priori knowledge of reality is possible
- **Key Figures**: Descartes, Spinoza, Leibniz
- **Primary Texts**: Meditations (Descartes), Ethics (Spinoza), Monadology (Leibniz)
- **Domains**: Epistemology, Metaphysics
- **Related Traditions**: Platonic, Cartesian

### Empiricism
- **Tradition ID**: `EMPIRICISM`
- **Period**: 17th-18th century
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Experience is the primary source of knowledge
  - No innate ideas; mind begins as tabula rasa
  - Concepts derive from sensory impressions
  - Skepticism about metaphysical claims beyond experience
- **Key Figures**: Locke, Berkeley, Hume
- **Primary Texts**: Essay Concerning Human Understanding (Locke), Treatise of Human Nature (Hume)
- **Domains**: Epistemology, Philosophy of Mind
- **Related Traditions**: Positivism, Pragmatism

### Kantian Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `KANTIAN`
- **Founder**: Immanuel Kant (1724-1804)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Synthesis of rationalism and empiricism
  - Phenomena (appearances) vs. noumena (things-in-themselves)
  - Transcendental idealism: mind structures experience
  - Categorical imperative: act only on universalizable maxims
  - Autonomy and dignity of rational beings
- **Key Figures**: Kant, Fichte, Schelling (early)
- **Primary Texts**: Critique of Pure Reason, Critique of Practical Reason, Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals
- **Domains**: Epistemology, Ethics, Aesthetics, Metaphysics
- **Related Traditions**: German Idealism, Deontological Ethics, Neo-Kantianism

## 19th Century Philosophy

### German Idealism
- **Period**: Late 18th - early 19th century
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Reality is fundamentally mental/spiritual
  - Dialectical development of thought and history
  - Absolute as self-knowing spirit
- **Key Figures**: Fichte, Schelling, Hegel
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Philosophy of History

### Hegelian Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `HEGELIAN`
- **Founder**: G.W.F. Hegel (1770-1831)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Dialectical method: thesis-antithesis-synthesis
  - Absolute Idealism: reality is the self-development of Spirit (Geist)
  - History has rational structure and direction
  - The real is rational; the rational is real
  - Recognition and intersubjectivity
- **Key Figures**: Hegel, Marx (in part), British Idealists, Alexandre Kojève
- **Primary Texts**: Phenomenology of Spirit, Science of Logic, Philosophy of Right
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Philosophy of History, Political Philosophy
- **Related Traditions**: Marxism, British Idealism, Frankfurt School

### Nietzschean Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `NIETZSCHEAN`
- **Founder**: Friedrich Nietzsche (1844-1900)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Death of God: collapse of traditional values
  - Will to power as fundamental drive
  - Eternal recurrence as existential test
  - Master vs. slave morality
  - Übermensch: self-overcoming and value creation
  - Perspectivism: no view from nowhere
- **Key Figures**: Nietzsche, Foucault, Deleuze
- **Primary Texts**: Thus Spoke Zarathustra, Beyond Good and Evil, Genealogy of Morality
- **Domains**: Ethics, Metaphysics, Aesthetics
- **Related Traditions**: Existentialism, Postmodernism

### Utilitarianism
- **Period**: 18th-19th century
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Greatest good for the greatest number
  - Consequences determine moral worth
  - Pleasure/happiness as intrinsic good
- **Key Figures**: Bentham, Mill, Sidgwick, Singer
- **Primary Texts**: Utilitarianism (Mill), Principles of Morals and Legislation (Bentham)
- **Domains**: Ethics, Political Philosophy

## 20th Century Philosophy

### Phenomenology
- **Tradition ID**: `PHENOMENOLOGY`
- **Founder**: Edmund Husserl (1859-1938)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Return to the things themselves
  - Intentionality: consciousness is always of something
  - Epoché: bracket assumptions to reveal essential structures
  - Lifeworld (Lebenswelt) as ground of all experience
  - Eidetic reduction: discern invariant essences
- **Key Figures**: Husserl, Heidegger, Merleau-Ponty, Sartre, Levinas
- **Primary Texts**: Logical Investigations, Ideas, Being and Time, Phenomenology of Perception
- **Domains**: Epistemology, Metaphysics, Philosophy of Mind
- **Related Traditions**: Existentialism, Hermeneutics

### Existentialism
- **Tradition ID**: `EXISTENTIALISM`
- **Period**: 19th-20th century
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Existence precedes essence
  - Radical freedom and responsibility
  - Authenticity vs. bad faith
  - Anxiety, absurdity, and finitude as fundamental
  - Subjective truth and personal commitment
- **Key Figures**: Kierkegaard, Heidegger, Sartre, Camus, de Beauvoir, Marcel
- **Primary Texts**: Being and Nothingness, The Myth of Sisyphus, Either/Or
- **Domains**: Ethics, Metaphysics, Philosophy of Religion
- **Related Traditions**: Phenomenology, Religious Existentialism

### Analytic Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `ANALYTIC`
- **Period**: 20th century - present
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Clarity and precision in philosophical language
  - Logical analysis as primary method
  - Close attention to language and meaning
  - Engagement with natural sciences
  - Breaking complex problems into simpler parts
- **Key Figures**: Frege, Russell, Moore, Wittgenstein, Quine, Kripke, Davidson
- **Primary Texts**: Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus, Philosophical Investigations, Word and Object
- **Domains**: Philosophy of Language, Logic, Philosophy of Mind, Metaphysics
- **Related Traditions**: Logical Positivism, Ordinary Language Philosophy

### Pragmatism
- **Tradition ID**: `PRAGMATISM`
- **Founders**: Charles Sanders Peirce (1839-1914), William James (1842-1910)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Meaning tied to practical consequences
  - Truth as what works in experience
  - Inquiry as problem-solving
  - Anti-foundationalism
  - Fallibilism: all beliefs revisable
- **Key Figures**: Peirce, James, Dewey, Rorty, Putnam
- **Primary Texts**: Pragmatism (James), Experience and Nature (Dewey)
- **Domains**: Epistemology, Philosophy of Science, Ethics
- **Related Traditions**: American Philosophy, Neopragmatism

### Continental Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `CONTINENTAL`
- **Period**: 20th century - present
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Critique of modernity and Enlightenment
  - Hermeneutics and interpretation
  - Historical and social context of thought
  - Engagement with art, literature, politics
- **Key Figures**: Heidegger, Gadamer, Derrida, Foucault, Deleuze, Habermas
- **Domains**: Social Philosophy, Aesthetics, Philosophy of History
- **Branches**: Hermeneutics, Critical Theory, Deconstruction, Poststructuralism

### Process Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `PROCESS_PHILOSOPHY`
- **Founder**: Alfred North Whitehead (1861-1947)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Reality is fundamentally processual, not substantial
  - Actual occasions of experience as basic units
  - Creativity as ultimate category
  - God as dipolar: primordial and consequent natures
  - Prehension: all entities feel each other
- **Key Figures**: Whitehead, Hartshorne, David Ray Griffin
- **Primary Texts**: Process and Reality, Adventures of Ideas
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Philosophy of Religion, Philosophy of Science
- **Related Traditions**: Panentheism, Ecological Philosophy

## Cross-Traditional Connections

### Perennial Themes

| Theme | Traditions Engaged |
|-------|-------------------|
| Nature of Being | Platonic, Aristotelian, Thomism, Hegelian, Phenomenology, Process |
| Knowledge & Skepticism | Empiricism, Rationalism, Kantian, Pragmatism, Analytic |
| Ethics & Virtue | Aristotelian, Stoicism, Kantian, Utilitarianism, Existentialism |
| Freedom & Determinism | Stoicism, Kantian, Existentialism, Analytic |
| Mind & Consciousness | Aristotelian, Phenomenology, Analytic, Process |
| Language & Meaning | Analytic, Continental, Pragmatism |
| Political Order | Platonic, Aristotelian, Kantian, Hegelian, Continental |

### Complementary Pairings

- **Stoicism + Existentialism**: Both address living authentically amid uncertainty
- **Phenomenology + Analytic**: Rigor meets first-person investigation
- **Pragmatism + Process**: Dynamic, anti-substantialist worldviews
- **Aristotelian + Thomism**: Virtue ethics with theological grounding
- **Kantian + Hegelian**: Transcendental critique meets historical development
