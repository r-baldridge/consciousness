# Eastern Philosophical Traditions Index

## Overview

This document provides a comprehensive index of Eastern philosophical traditions integrated into Form 28. Each tradition is catalogued with its core tenets, key figures, primary texts, and relationships to other schools. These traditions offer rich perspectives on consciousness, ethics, metaphysics, and liberation that complement and challenge Western approaches.

---

## Buddhist Philosophy

### Theravada Buddhism
- **Tradition ID**: `BUDDHIST_THERAVADA`
- **Origin**: Sri Lanka and Southeast Asia, preserving early Buddhist teachings
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Four Noble Truths: suffering, its origin, its cessation, the path
  - Noble Eightfold Path: right view, intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration
  - Three Marks of Existence: impermanence (anicca), suffering (dukkha), non-self (anatta)
  - Dependent origination (paticcasamuppada)
  - Nibbana as cessation of craving and liberation
  - Abhidhamma: detailed analysis of mental and physical phenomena
- **Key Figures**: Buddhaghosa, Mahasi Sayadaw, Ajahn Chah
- **Primary Texts**: Pali Canon (Tipitaka), Visuddhimagga
- **Domains**: Philosophy of Mind, Ethics, Phenomenology, Meditation
- **Related Traditions**: Early Buddhism, Insight Meditation

### Mahayana Buddhism
- **Tradition ID**: `BUDDHIST_MAHAYANA`
- **Origin**: India, spreading to East Asia
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Bodhisattva ideal: liberation for all beings
  - Emptiness (sunyata): all phenomena lack inherent existence
  - Buddha-nature: enlightenment potential in all beings
  - Two truths: conventional and ultimate
  - Skillful means (upaya): adaptive teaching methods
  - Compassion (karuna) as equal to wisdom (prajna)
- **Key Figures**: Nagarjuna, Asanga, Vasubandhu, Shantideva
- **Primary Texts**: Prajnaparamita Sutras, Madhyamaka texts, Yogacara treatises
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Ethics, Philosophy of Mind
- **Related Traditions**: Madhyamaka, Yogacara, East Asian Buddhism

#### Madhyamaka (Middle Way)
- **Founder**: Nagarjuna (c. 150-250 CE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Emptiness of all phenomena (sunyata)
  - Refutation of all extreme views through prasanga (reductio)
  - Dependent origination equals emptiness
  - Two truths: conventional designation and ultimate emptiness
  - Middle way between eternalism and nihilism
- **Key Figures**: Nagarjuna, Aryadeva, Candrakirti, Tsongkhapa
- **Primary Texts**: Mulamadhyamakakarika, Madhyamakavatara
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Logic

#### Yogacara (Mind-Only)
- **Founders**: Asanga, Vasubandhu (4th-5th century CE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Three natures: imagined, dependent, perfected
  - Eight consciousnesses including alaya-vijnana (storehouse consciousness)
  - External objects are projections of mind
  - Transformation of consciousness into wisdom
  - Detailed phenomenology of mental processes
- **Key Figures**: Asanga, Vasubandhu, Dignaga, Dharmakirti
- **Primary Texts**: Trimsika, Madhyantavibhaga, Abhidharmasamuccaya
- **Domains**: Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology, Phenomenology

### Vajrayana Buddhism
- **Tradition ID**: `BUDDHIST_VAJRAYANA`
- **Origin**: India, flourishing in Tibet and Mongolia
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Tantric methods for rapid enlightenment
  - Transformation of afflictions into wisdom
  - Deity yoga and visualization practices
  - Guru devotion as essential
  - Unity of method and wisdom
  - Ground, path, and fruition structure
- **Key Figures**: Padmasambhava, Milarepa, Tsongkhapa, Longchenpa
- **Primary Texts**: Guhyasamaja Tantra, Treasury of Knowledge (Kongtrul)
- **Domains**: Soteriology, Philosophy of Mind, Ethics
- **Related Traditions**: Tibetan Buddhism, Shingon

### Zen Buddhism
- **Tradition ID**: `BUDDHIST_ZEN`
- **Origin**: China (Chan), spreading to Japan, Korea, Vietnam
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Direct pointing at mind; seeing one's nature (kensho)
  - Transmission outside scriptures
  - Sudden vs. gradual awakening debates
  - Zazen (just sitting) as primary practice
  - Koans as vehicles for breakthrough
  - Ordinary mind is the Way
  - Non-duality of practice and enlightenment
- **Key Figures**: Bodhidharma, Huineng, Dogen, Hakuin, Linji (Rinzai)
- **Primary Texts**: Platform Sutra, Mumonkan, Shobogenzo, Blue Cliff Record
- **Domains**: Philosophy of Mind, Phenomenology, Aesthetics
- **Related Traditions**: Chan, Seon (Korean), Thien (Vietnamese)

## Chinese Philosophy

### Daoism (Taoism)
- **Tradition ID**: `DAOIST`
- **Founders**: Laozi (legendary), Zhuangzi (c. 369-286 BCE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Dao (Way): ineffable source and pattern of all things
  - Wu-wei: non-action, effortless action, acting without forcing
  - Naturalness (ziran): spontaneous alignment with nature
  - Yin-yang: complementary opposites in dynamic balance
  - Return to simplicity (pu)
  - Relativity of conventional distinctions
  - Te (virtue/power): natural efficacy from alignment with Dao
- **Key Figures**: Laozi, Zhuangzi, Liezi, Wang Bi, Ge Hong
- **Primary Texts**: Daodejing (Tao Te Ching), Zhuangzi, Liezi
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Ethics, Political Philosophy, Aesthetics
- **Related Traditions**: Neo-Daoism, Religious Daoism, Internal Alchemy

### Confucianism
- **Tradition ID**: `CONFUCIAN`
- **Founder**: Kongzi/Confucius (551-479 BCE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Ren: humaneness, benevolence, love
  - Li: ritual propriety, social norms
  - Yi: righteousness, duty
  - Junzi: the exemplary person, moral cultivation
  - Filial piety (xiao): respect for parents and ancestors
  - Rectification of names: language-reality correspondence
  - Harmony through hierarchy and role fulfillment
- **Key Figures**: Confucius, Mencius, Xunzi, Dong Zhongshu
- **Primary Texts**: Analects, Mencius, Xunzi, Classic of Rites
- **Domains**: Ethics, Political Philosophy, Education
- **Related Traditions**: Neo-Confucianism, New Confucianism

### Neo-Confucianism
- **Tradition ID**: `NEO_CONFUCIAN`
- **Period**: Song Dynasty (960-1279) onwards
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Li (principle) and qi (material force) cosmology
  - Mind-heart (xin) cultivation
  - Investigation of things (gewu)
  - Unity of knowledge and action (Wang Yangming)
  - Innate moral knowing (liangzhi)
  - Integration of Buddhist/Daoist insights into Confucian framework
- **Key Figures**: Zhou Dunyi, Cheng Yi, Cheng Hao, Zhu Xi, Wang Yangming, Lu Xiangshan
- **Primary Texts**: Reflections on Things at Hand, Instructions for Practical Living
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Ethics, Epistemology
- **Related Traditions**: Confucian, Korean/Japanese Neo-Confucianism

### Chinese Buddhism
- **Period**: 1st century CE onwards
- **Distinctive Features**:
  - Sinification of Indian Buddhism
  - Buddha-nature emphasis
  - Harmonization with Daoist/Confucian thought
  - Development of Chan, Tiantai, Huayan schools
- **Key Figures**: Kumarajiva, Zhiyi, Fazang, Huineng
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Philosophy of Mind

## Indian Philosophy (Darshanas)

### Vedantic Philosophy

#### Advaita Vedanta
- **Tradition ID**: `VEDANTIC_ADVAITA`
- **Founder**: Shankara (c. 788-820 CE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Non-dualism: Brahman alone is real
  - World (maya) is appearance, neither real nor unreal
  - Atman (self) is identical with Brahman
  - Ignorance (avidya) causes bondage
  - Liberation through knowledge (jnana)
  - Three levels of reality: absolute, empirical, illusory
  - Tat tvam asi: "That thou art"
- **Key Figures**: Shankara, Gaudapada, Suresvara, Vidyaranya, Ramana Maharshi
- **Primary Texts**: Brahma Sutras (with commentary), Upanishads, Vivekacudamani
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Epistemology, Soteriology
- **Related Traditions**: Upanishadic, Neo-Vedanta

#### Vishishtadvaita (Qualified Non-Dualism)
- **Tradition ID**: `VEDANTIC_DVAITA` (note: covers qualified non-dualism as alternative to pure non-dualism)
- **Founder**: Ramanuja (1017-1137 CE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Brahman is qualified by souls and matter
  - World is real, the body of Brahman
  - Individual souls are eternally distinct
  - Devotion (bhakti) as path to liberation
  - Grace of Vishnu essential
- **Key Figures**: Ramanuja, Vedanta Desika
- **Primary Texts**: Sri Bhashya, Vedartha Sangraha
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Philosophy of Religion

#### Dvaita (Dualism)
- **Founder**: Madhva (1238-1317 CE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Five-fold difference: God-soul, God-matter, soul-soul, soul-matter, matter-matter
  - Brahman (Vishnu) is independent; all else dependent
  - Souls eternally distinct from God
  - Liberation is eternal service to God in Vaikuntha
- **Key Figures**: Madhva, Jayatirtha
- **Primary Texts**: Anuvyakhyana, Brahma Sutra Bhashya
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Theology

### Samkhya
- **Tradition ID**: `SAMKHYA`
- **Traditional Founder**: Kapila
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Dualism of consciousness (purusha) and matter (prakriti)
  - Prakriti evolves into 23 tattvas (categories)
  - Three gunas: sattva, rajas, tamas
  - Liberation through discrimination (viveka)
  - Purushas are many, inactive witnesses
- **Key Figures**: Kapila, Ishvarakrishna
- **Primary Texts**: Samkhya Karika
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Philosophy of Mind

### Yoga Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `YOGA_PHILOSOPHY`
- **Founder**: Patanjali (c. 400 CE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Yoga is cessation of mental fluctuations (citta-vritti-nirodha)
  - Eight limbs: yama, niyama, asana, pranayama, pratyahara, dharana, dhyana, samadhi
  - Kaivalya: isolation of purusha from prakriti
  - Theistic addition to Samkhya framework
  - Detailed phenomenology of mental states
- **Key Figures**: Patanjali, Vyasa
- **Primary Texts**: Yoga Sutras, Yoga Bhashya
- **Domains**: Philosophy of Mind, Phenomenology, Soteriology

### Nyaya (Logic)
- **Tradition ID**: `NYAYA`
- **Founder**: Gautama (c. 2nd century BCE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Pramanas (valid means of knowledge): perception, inference, comparison, testimony
  - Syllogistic logic with five-membered arguments
  - Realism about external world
  - Liberation through right knowledge
  - Detailed categories of reality
- **Key Figures**: Gautama, Vatsyayana, Udayana, Gangesa
- **Primary Texts**: Nyaya Sutras, Nyaya Bhashya, Tattvacintamani
- **Domains**: Logic, Epistemology

### Vaisheshika (Atomism)
- **Tradition ID**: `VAISHESHIKA`
- **Founder**: Kanada (c. 2nd century BCE)
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Six/seven categories: substance, quality, action, universal, particularity, inherence, (absence)
  - Atomic theory of matter
  - Nine substances including space, time, mind, soul
  - Liberation through knowledge of categories
- **Key Figures**: Kanada, Prashastapada
- **Primary Texts**: Vaisheshika Sutras
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Physics

### Jain Philosophy
- **Tradition ID**: `JAIN`
- **Founder**: Mahavira (599-527 BCE), building on earlier Tirthankaras
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Anekantavada: many-sidedness of reality
  - Syadvada: conditional predication ("in some sense...")
  - Ahimsa: non-violence as supreme duty
  - Karma as material substance binding soul
  - Three jewels: right faith, knowledge, conduct
  - Soul (jiva) vs. non-soul (ajiva)
  - Liberation through asceticism and knowledge
- **Key Figures**: Mahavira, Umasvati, Kundakunda, Haribhadra
- **Primary Texts**: Tattvartha Sutra, Agamas
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Ethics, Epistemology, Logic

## Japanese Philosophy

### Japanese Buddhism
- **Distinctive Schools**:
  - **Tendai**: Comprehensive system, all teachings lead to one vehicle
  - **Shingon**: Esoteric Buddhism, mantra and mandala practices
  - **Pure Land**: Nembutsu practice, Other Power (tariki)
  - **Nichiren**: Lotus Sutra devotion
  - **Zen**: Rinzai (koan) and Soto (shikantaza)
- **Key Figures**: Saicho, Kukai, Honen, Shinran, Dogen, Hakuin
- **Domains**: Philosophy of Religion, Ethics, Aesthetics

### Kyoto School
- **Period**: 20th century
- **Core Tenets**:
  - Dialogue between Eastern thought and Western philosophy
  - Absolute Nothingness (zettai mu)
  - Self-awakening and religious consciousness
  - Logic of place (basho)
- **Key Figures**: Nishida Kitaro, Tanabe Hajime, Nishitani Keiji
- **Primary Texts**: An Inquiry into the Good, Religion and Nothingness
- **Domains**: Metaphysics, Philosophy of Religion

## Cross-Traditional Connections

### East-West Bridges

| Eastern Concept | Western Parallel | Notes |
|-----------------|------------------|-------|
| Sunyata (Emptiness) | Negative Theology, Deconstruction | Both negate fixed essence |
| Buddha-nature | Platonic Form of Good | Intrinsic perfection |
| Wu-wei | Stoic acceptance | Alignment with natural order |
| Karma | Moral causation | Action-consequence linkage |
| Dependent origination | Process philosophy | Relational ontology |
| Advaita non-dualism | Spinoza's monism | Ultimate unity |
| Yogacara mind-only | Idealism | Mind-primacy |
| Jain anekantavada | Perspectivism | Multiple valid views |

### Intra-Eastern Dialogues

- **Buddhist-Hindu Debates**: Self (atman) vs. no-self (anatman)
- **Confucian-Daoist Tensions**: Social cultivation vs. natural spontaneity
- **Madhyamaka-Yogacara**: Emptiness vs. mind-only
- **Chinese Buddhism**: Synthesis of Indian Buddhism with native thought

### Convergent Themes

| Theme | Traditions Engaged |
|-------|-------------------|
| Non-dual awareness | Advaita, Zen, Daoism, Madhyamaka |
| Ethical cultivation | Confucian, Buddhist, Jain, Yoga |
| Liberation/enlightenment | All Indian traditions, Buddhism |
| Nature of mind | Yogacara, Zen, Samkhya, Yoga |
| Causation and change | Buddhist, Samkhya, Vaisheshika |
| Logic and epistemology | Nyaya, Buddhist logic, Jain |
