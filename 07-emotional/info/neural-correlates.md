## Summary of Key Empirical Findings

### Critical Neural Correlates for Emotional Consciousness
1. **Amygdala Complex**: Rapid emotional evaluation, threat detection, emotional significance assessment
2. **Prefrontal Cortex**: Conscious emotion regulation, emotional working memory, meta-emotional awareness
3. **Limbic System Integration**: Emotional memory formation, autonomic awareness, reward consciousness
4. **Neurotransmitter Systems**: Dopaminergic reward consciousness, serotonergic emotional homeostasis

### Electrophysiological Markers
1. **Early Components (100-200ms)**: Pre-conscious emotional processing
2. **Late Components (300-800ms)**: Conscious emotional awareness
3. **Oscillatory Activity**: Theta (emotional memory), Gamma (emotional binding)

### Clinical Evidence
1. **Lesion Studies**: Demonstrate necessity of specific brain regions for emotional consciousness
2. **Psychiatric Disorders**: Show altered emotional consciousness with neural dysfunction
3. **Developmental Studies**: Reveal gradual emergence of emotional consciousness with brain maturation

This empirical foundation provides the necessary neural correlates and mechanisms for implementing artificial emotional consciousness systems.
