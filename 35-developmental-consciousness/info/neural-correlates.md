## Conclusion

This analysis of neural correlates of developmental consciousness provides essential constraints:

1. **Prenatal Threshold**: Consciousness requires thalamocortical connectivity (~24 weeks)
2. **Birth Transition**: Neurochemical surge enables consciousness emergence at birth
3. **Synaptic Development**: Overproduction then pruning shapes consciousness circuits
4. **Prefrontal Maturation**: Full maturation by age 25 enables executive consciousness
5. **Hippocampal Development**: Hippocampal maturation enables autobiographical consciousness
6. **Mentalizing Network**: Theory of mind requires specific network development
7. **Aging Changes**: Compensation mechanisms preserve consciousness despite decline
8. **Terminal States**: Dying brain shows complex patterns relevant to consciousness

These neural constraints provide the empirical foundation for implementing developmental consciousness trajectories in artificial systems.
