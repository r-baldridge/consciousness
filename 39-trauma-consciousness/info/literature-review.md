## Overview
This document presents a comprehensive literature review of trauma consciousness research, including PTSD studies, dissociation research, and the phenomenology of trauma-altered states. The review synthesizes research from trauma neuroscience, clinical psychology, affective neuroscience, and consciousness studies to inform understanding of how traumatic experiences fundamentally alter conscious experience.

This review takes a **trauma-informed, survivor-centered** approach, recognizing that protective mechanisms developed in response to overwhelming experience represent the nervous system's wisdom, not pathology.

## Summary and Implications for Trauma Consciousness Modeling

This literature review establishes that trauma fundamentally alters consciousness through:

1. **Neurobiological mechanisms** affecting brain structure, function, and chemistry
2. **Dissociative adaptations** that fragment and protect consciousness
3. **Autonomic nervous system dysregulation** creating altered states of arousal
4. **Memory system disruptions** affecting how experience is encoded and retrieved
5. **Self and identity alterations** changing the fundamental experience of being

The research supports a model of trauma consciousness that:
- Honors protective mechanisms as adaptive
- Recognizes the body's central role in trauma and healing
- Acknowledges multiple pathways to recovery
- Integrates biological, psychological, and relational perspectives
- Maintains hope for healing and post-traumatic growth

---

*This literature review informs the development of trauma-informed computational models while maintaining deep respect for the lived experience of trauma survivors.*
