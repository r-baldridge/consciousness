## Conclusion

This theoretical frameworks document establishes the foundational theories guiding folk wisdom implementation:

1. **Relational Epistemology**: Knowledge understood as constituted through relationships with known entities, requiring relational architecture
2. **Oral Epistemology**: Narrative, performative, and formulaic modes of knowing requiring multimodal representation
3. **New Animism**: Expanded personhood and relational ethics requiring consciousness systems capable of non-human person representation
4. **Perspectivism**: Multiple valid perspectives requiring flexible ontological representation
5. **Indigenous Cosmology**: Multi-world structures and temporal modes requiring comprehensive cosmological modeling
6. **Traditional Ethics**: Reciprocity-based relational ethics requiring value-integrated processing
7. **Wisdom Application**: Contextual, adaptive application requiring sophisticated analogical reasoning

These theoretical frameworks provide principled guidance for implementing artificial folk wisdom systems that can authentically engage with traditional knowledge while maintaining cultural sensitivity, attribution integrity, and ethical responsibility.
