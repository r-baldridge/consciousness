# Form 26: Split-brain Consciousness - Literature Review

## Historical Foundations

### Early Clinical Observations

**Van Wagenen and Herren (1940)**
- First systematic corpus callosotomy procedures for epilepsy treatment
- Initial observations of minimal behavioral changes in daily life
- Foundation for understanding that hemispheric disconnection could be therapeutically viable
- Limited testing revealed subtle coordination difficulties

**Akelaitis (1941-1944)**
- Comprehensive neurological assessments of early callosotomy patients
- Documentation of preserved language and basic cognitive functions
- Observations of subtle visual field integration difficulties
- Establishment of surgical safety and general cognitive preservation

### Pioneering Experimental Research

**Sperry and Gazzaniga (1960s-1970s)**
- Revolutionary controlled experimental paradigms
- Development of tachistoscopic testing methods
- Discovery of hemispheric specialization and independence
- Demonstration of dual consciousness phenomena

**Key Findings from Sperry's Laboratory**:
- Left hemisphere: Language dominant, analytical, sequential processing
- Right hemisphere: Spatial processing, pattern recognition, emotional processing
- Independent learning and memory formation in each hemisphere
- Conflicting responses and preferences between hemispheres

**Gazzaniga's "Interpreter" Theory (1970s-present)**
- Left hemisphere as the "interpreter" creating coherent narratives
- Confabulation and rationalization of right hemisphere actions
- Understanding of how unity of consciousness is constructed
- Implications for normal consciousness and self-awareness

## Contemporary Research Developments

**Paul et al. (2007)**

- Evidence for independent hemispheric activation patterns

- Subcortical pathways maintaining some integration
