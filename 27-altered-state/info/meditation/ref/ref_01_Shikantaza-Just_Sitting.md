# Reference: Shikantaza - Just Sitting

## Core Concepts

### Essential Nature of Shikantaza
- **Pure sitting**: Meditation without object, goal, or technique beyond sitting itself
- **Non-directed awareness**: Open presence that doesn't focus on any particular phenomenon
- **Buddha-nature expression**: Sitting as the natural manifestation of enlightened awareness
- **Goalless practice**: Understanding that the practice itself is the realization

### Meaning and Significance
- **Literal translation**: "Just sitting" (Japanese: shikan-taza)
- **Dogen's innovation**: Central practice of Soto Zen as taught by Dogen Zenji
- **Being-practice**: Practice that is the expression rather than the means to enlightenment
- **Ordinary extraordinariness**: Recognition that Buddha-nature is completely natural

### Five-Posture Foundation
1. **Head**: Slightly tucked, eyes half-closed in natural gaze
2. **Spine**: Naturally erect without forcing or straining
3. **Shoulders**: Relaxed and naturally positioned
4. **Hands**: Cosmic mudra (hands forming oval with thumbs lightly touching)
5. **Legs**: Full lotus, half lotus, seiza, or chair sitting with feet flat

### Core Attitude
- **Just this**: Complete presence to whatever is arising in this moment
- **No gaining mind**: Sitting without seeking any particular state or experience
- **Allowing everything**: Permitting all thoughts, emotions, and sensations to be as they are
- **Natural awareness**: Resting in the inherent clarity of consciousness

## Practical Applications

### Step-by-Step Instructions
1. **Posture establishment**: Arrange the five-posture foundation with care and attention
2. **Breath settlement**: Allow natural breathing without control or manipulation
3. **Open awareness**: Rest in choiceless awareness of whatever arises
4. **Thought management**: Neither suppress nor follow thoughts - simply let them be
5. **Session completion**: End with appreciation for the natural clarity that was always present

### Working with Mental Activity
- **Thought arising**: Notice thoughts without judgment or resistance
- **Emotional waves**: Allow feelings to arise and pass without interference
- **Sensation awareness**: Include all bodily sensations in open awareness
- **Return to sitting**: Gently come back to simple presence when caught in mental content

### Progressive Development
1. **Stabilization**: Learning to sit with minimal distraction or effort
2. **Deepening**: Increasing periods of natural, effortless awareness
3. **Integration**: Carrying "just sitting" mind into daily activities
4. **Mature practice**: Complete ordinariness of enlightened sitting

### Session Structure
- **Beginning**: Brief settling with few conscious breaths
- **Main practice**: Extended period of open, goalless sitting
- **Closing**: Gentle transition back to activity with gratitude

- **Natural perfection**: Recognition that consciousness is already complete

- **Ordinary Buddha-nature**: Understanding that awakeness is completely natural

- **Practice-enlightenment unity**: Sitting zazen is itself the manifestation of Buddha-nature
- **Original enlightenment**: Recognition that awakening has always been present
- **Dropping body and mind**: Complete release of identification with physical and mental phenomena
- **Being-time**: Understanding that true existence is always now

- **Purposeless purpose**: Sitting without goal to realize the goalless nature of being

- **Becoming what you are**: Realizing the Buddha-nature that was never absent

1. **Daily sitting**: Consistent practice at the same time and place
2. **Posture attention**: Careful establishment of the five-posture foundation
3. **Open awareness**: Maintaining choiceless presence to whatever arises
4. **Non-interference**: Allowing all mental and physical phenomena to be as they are

### Signs of Mature Practice
- **Effortless sitting**: Natural ease in maintaining posture and awareness
- **Thought transparency**: Mental content arising and passing without disturbance

### Common Obstacles and Solutions
- **Goal orientation**: Remember that shikantaza has no purpose beyond itself
- **Mental resistance**: Allow thoughts and emotions without fighting or following

- **Boredom or restlessness**: Include all experiences in the open embrace of just sitting
- **Doubt**: Trust in the natural perfection that doesn't need improvement
