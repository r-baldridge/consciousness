## Overview

This literature review examines the scientific foundations of neurodivergent consciousness, providing the theoretical basis for understanding and modeling diverse cognitive processing modes. Neurodivergent consciousness represents the rich variety of ways human brains can be structured and function - recognizing neurological differences as natural variations in human cognition rather than deficits to be corrected.

### Key Insights for Implementation

1. **Diversity in Processing**: No single model captures all neurodivergent experience
2. **Strengths Recognition**: Each neurotype has distinct cognitive advantages
3. **Context Dependency**: Functioning varies significantly with environmental fit
4. **Individual Variation**: Substantial differences exist within each neurotype
5. **First-Person Authority**: Lived experience must inform understanding

This literature review establishes the theoretical foundation for implementing artificial systems that can understand, model, and support neurodivergent consciousness in its full diversity and richness.
