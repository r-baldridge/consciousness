# Literature Review: Plant Intelligence & Vegetal Consciousness

## Overview
This literature review examines the theoretical foundations of plant intelligence and vegetal consciousness, providing the scientific basis for implementing plant cognition models within consciousness systems. Plant intelligence represents a fundamental challenge to neurobiological assumptions about cognition - demonstrating that learning, memory, decision-making, and communication can emerge without neurons.

## Conclusion

This literature review establishes the theoretical foundation for implementing artificial plant intelligence systems. Key findings include:

1. **Distributed Processing**: Plants achieve cognitive functions through distributed networks rather than centralized brains
2. **Learning and Memory**: Rigorous experiments demonstrate plant learning meeting criteria from animal cognition research
3. **Communication Networks**: Plants communicate through volatile, chemical, electrical, and mycorrhizal channels
4. **Decision Making**: Plants make sophisticated decisions involving cost-benefit analysis and risk assessment
5. **Indigenous Knowledge**: Traditional knowledge systems provide millennia of plant behavior observations
6. **Philosophical Implications**: Plant intelligence challenges assumptions about neural requirements for cognition

The literature provides clear guidance for implementing plant intelligence models that capture the essential features of vegetal cognition while remaining grounded in empirical evidence and respectful of traditional knowledge systems.
